Idea Proposal #1

Amelia Kim

How do economic downturns affect investment decisions in the U.S. economy?

Tractable Data

  • Public, aggregate economic data that captures overall investment behavior in the U.S. economy
  • Investment and economic conditions are measured at a broad level, making the data easy to manage and interpret
  • The scope of the data focuses on data that is publicly available and does not rely on private investments.

Data Retrieval

  • Investment measures and economic indicators are available through government and research institutions
  • Private Fixed Investment- Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA), Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)
  • NBER Recession Indicator- National Bureau of Economic Research, FRED

Correct Specification of the Model

  • Analysis will compare investment behavior during periods of economic downturn to periods of economic stability
  • Model designed to examine whether changes in economic conditions are associated with changes in investment decisions

Implications for Stakeholders

  • Can help explain how investment behavior responds to economic uncertainty
  • Policymakers can use this information to better understand the timing and magnitude of investment slowdowns
  • The results provide insight into why economic recoveries may take time following downturns

Ethical, Legal, and Societal Implications

  • Reduced investment during downturns can have long-term effects on employment and economic growth
  • Prolonged investment slowdowns may widen existing economic inequalities
  • The analysis highlights the broader social importance of policies that encourage stable investment during periods of uncertainty
